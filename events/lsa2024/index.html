LSA 2024 Session: Formal Language Theory in Morphology and Phonology

On January 6, 2024, there was a session held on Formal Language Theory in Morphology and Phonology at the 100th meeting of the LSA organized by Jeffrey Heinz, Scott Nelson, and Jon Rawski. This webpage is a record of who presented what there. Session Abstract

Tutorial

  • Jane Chandlee, Adam Jardine and Scott Nelson. Tutorial: Logic and Model Theory for Phonology.

Session 1

  • Tatevik Yolyan. Weak Determinism and Simultaneous Application via Boolean Monadic Recursive Schemes.
  • Connor Mayer. One (semi)ring to rule them all: Reconciling categorical and gradient models of phonotactics

Session 2

  • Andrew Lamont. Optimality Theory is not computable.
  • Yang Wang. What does formal language theory tell us about the nature of reduplication?

Session 3

  • Magda Markowska. EMpirical and Theoretical Arguments for Using Phonological Features for the Learning of Subsequential Functions
  • Caleb Belth. The Interaction Between Learning Algorithms and Formal Language Theory
