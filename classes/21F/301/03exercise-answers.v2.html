The phonological generalizations would be something like:

  • When [n] is followed by a consonant, its place of articulation changes to match the articulation of the following consonant. In other words, [n] becomes [m] before [p] and [b]; and [n] becomes [ŋ] before [k] and [g]. Let's call this generalization Nasal Place Assimilation.

  • When a voiced stop is preceded by a nasal consonant, the voiced stop becomes the nasal of the matching place of articulation. In other words, [b] becomes [m] before [n] and [m] and [ŋ]; and [d] becomes [n] before [n] and [m] and [ŋ]; and [g] becomes [ŋ] before [n] and [m] and [ŋ]. Let's call this generalization Nasalization.
