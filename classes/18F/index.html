29 Aug 2018

  • Today we went over regular expressions (REs), generalized regular expressions (GREs), star-free expressions (SFE), and "cat-union" expressions. [cm02.pdf]
  • We introduced epsilon transitions, which have an epsilon on the input side, and discussed how they also introduce non-determinism.
  • We mentioned the following theorems.
    • Kleene's theorem: The formal languages (sets of strings) describable with regular expressions are exactly the ones recognizable by finite-state acceptors.
    • The formal languages recognizable with non-deterministic acceptors are exactly the ones recognizable with determinisitc finite-state acceptors.
  • With the epsilon transitions, we explained how finite-state acceptors are closed under union, concatenation, and Kleene star.
